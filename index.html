The Project

What is Australian Political Sentiments?

A student-led data science project using the Twitter / X API to measure and visualise Australian public sentiment on key political issues. Follow the graphs at @publicmetricsAU.

Methodology

The data is collected through X API v2 and is in accordance with the platform guidelines.

Valence Aware Dictionary and sEntiment Reasoner (VADER) is used to estimate the sentiment on each post. The prebuilt Python library in natural language toolkit (nltk) is the variant used to approximate the sentiment.

There is a stronger weighing for posts that invoke a greater reaction from the environment suggesting that it is a "popular" opinion amongst Twitter users. The final weighing for each post is calculated by the sum of views multiplied by the probability of post engagement in the log scale. This attempts to reward posts with greater popularity without skewing the data through some outliers.

The Creator

About Me

I'm a high school student with a passion for data, technology, and Australian politics. Australian Political Sentiments started as a personal project to understand how Australians engage with political issues on social media.

I'm applying to volunteer with a local Victorian MP ahead of the 2026 state election, where I hope to develop this sentiment analyser further and contribute meaningful data insights to a real political campaign.
